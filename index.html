Shadow Place
In E-Scooter

The part I wanted to say is about the invisible impact of the scooter on our society and the pain of someone hidden behind convenience service. it is about the scooter's invisible shadow place part what we got to know. We thought the environmental problems of scooters were too easy to find and this shadow part is new problems we learned from further research. it is really interesting part for me. I wanted to communicate visually to help us understand the various stakeholders in society related to object that is not visible and I explained the stories of people in the shadow place that we learn.

How shared e-scooters
have impact our society

Platform Workers in a Labor BlindSpot

Shared electric scooter reduce loss by traffic jam, and enable efficient transportation. Their crucial service is highlighted by flexibility in pick-up, returning, and charging. Because they don’t have specified parking lot, users can leave them on streets after finding them with mobile applications. Transportation is no longer a belonging, but is rather becoming a concept of sharing. Electric scooters are very convenient and welcomed in that they have no troubles of loss or parking. electric scooters use battery for pick-up and drops without docking. They are charged by contract labors at night. These kind of charging communities are rapidly increasing with electric scooter service. We researched the shadow place of scooters and found that new social problems arise from the core services of scooters.

Lime Juicers who work at Lime as a subcontractor or freelancer are ‘ordinary people’ thet collects, recharges and returns scooter. Between 9pm and 7 am in the evening when service user is reduced, scooters are collected and recharged at each Juicer’s individual workplace, home, or office and place in the specified lime hub. Once a scooter is recovered, charged, or placed, the charger can earn between 2.5 euro to 4.5 euro per unit, depending on the amount of battery remaining and the location of the scooter to be collected. As platform based sharing economy such as Lime, economic activities based on sharing were activated and brought significant changes to the labor market such as break age of labor relations and expansion of platform workers. Reaching the ‘sharing economy’era, labor is being changed as a ‘voluntary’ and ‘enjoyable’ thing. Today, platform companies glorify labors that give money to platforms as not being a labor. Lime also seems to have created it is form of for people who seek to make a little side money through leftover space and walking, but recently ‘charger’ who work with a separate site and cars such as trucks and work tens of jobs per day have been emerging. This means that some Juicer work as their main job rather than a side job. Lime provides teens and young professionals the easy source of money and entertainment. But in some cities, they become too competitive and create illegal or violent actions to get scooters first. They also become the target of pickpockets.Even though it advertises with an easy saying “Anyone can make money easily”, But nobody takes responsibility if accidents occur while working. From companies perspective, platform labor costs less than formally hiring laborers, and Since the payment for the labor is not low compared to other works and people can choose the working hours, from profit sides, it can be favorable, but because platform labor (Juicer) works based on ‘autonomy’, it is difficult to be recognized as a laborer, so it has a lot of limitations. As mentioned earlier, they are exposed to a lot of risks, and bound to be instable. But there is no protection standards for ‘platform labors’, and it has become the biggest problem today.
Platform companies such as Lime are growing every day, yet the platform labors who led these businesses growth is becoming poor due to the payment, pressure of decline in labor conditions, instable income, ambiguity of legal regulations.
